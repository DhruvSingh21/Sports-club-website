Badminton is a fast-paced racket sport played both recreationally and competitively. It is typically played indoors on a rectangular court divided by a net. The game involves two or four players, with the objective of scoring points by hitting a shuttlecock (a feathered or synthetic projectile) over the net and into the opponent's side of the court. It offers a great way to stay physically active, improve one's fitness, and enjoy a competitive or recreational game. Whether played casually with friends or in a professional setting, badminton offers a combination of athleticism and fun.

Kabaddi

Fees:Rs 700 per Month

Timings: Sat-Wed,5:00 PM - 8:00 PM

Kabaddi is a contact team sport played between two teams of seven players, originating from India. The objective of the game is for a single player on offense, referred to as a "raider", to run into the opposing team's half of the court, touch out as many of their players and return to their own half of the court, all without being tackled by the defenders in 30 seconds. Points are scored for each player tagged by the raider, while the opposing team earns a point for stopping the raider. Players are taken out of the game if they are touched or tackled, but are brought back in for each point scored by their team from a tag or a tackle.
